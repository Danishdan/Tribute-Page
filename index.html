James J. Gibson

Ranked one of the 88th most cited psychologist of the 20th century

"The meaning or value of a thing consists of what it affords.”

― James J. Gibson, Reasons for Realism (1982)

Here is a timeline of James J. Gibson´s life:

  • 1904 - Born McConnelsville, Ohio.
  • 1928 - He receives his Ph.D. on the dissertation on A Study of the Reproduction of Visually Perceived Forms from Princeton University.
  • 1932 - He marries Eleanor Jack who he meets during his time teaching at Smith College. A prominent psychologist known for her investigations of what has been called the "visual cliff".
  • 1940 - Eleanor and James have their first child James Jerome Jr.
  • 1942 - He becomes a director of a unit in the Army Air Forces Aviation Psychology Program during World War II. During this time he becomes interested in visual perception.
  • 1943 - Eleanor and James have their second child Jean Grier.
  • 1946 - He was promoted to the rank of lieutenant colonel because of his work during the war.
  • 1947 - Sees the publication of central importance in the evolution of Gibson´s approach.
  • 1948 - Another publication of central importance in the evolution of Gibson´s approach is published.
  • 1950 - He publishes the book The Perception of the Visual World.
  • 1952 - The book wins the Warren Medal. He also becomes the president for the American Psychological Association.
  • 1961 - He receives the Distinguished Scientific Contribution Award, becoming a Fulbright fellow at Oxford University, a fellow of the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton University, and a fellow of the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University.
  • 1966 - He published The Sense Considered as Perceptual Systems.
  • 1967 - He received the honorary doctorates by Edinburgh and Uppsala University.
  • 1979 - He publishes the book The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception.
  • 1979 - Dies at the age of 75 Ithaca, New York.
